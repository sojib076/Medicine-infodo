## Indications
Nikethamide is indicated in- 

- Acute respiratory failure
- Respiratory depression following anesthesia
- CNS depression

## Pharmacology
Nikethamide also known as nicotinic acid diethylamide is a central & respiratory stimulant. It has direct stimulatory action on CNS, increases the frequency & depth of breathing. It has no immediate impact on the cardiovascular system. It is extensively metabolized in liver. All metabolites & a small amount of unchanged drug is excreted in urine.

## Dosage & Administration
2 to 10 mL by slow IV injection of the usual 25% solution every 20 to 30 minutes if necessary. Infants and children are particularly susceptible to toxic effects of nikethamide.

## Interaction
Anesthetic, sympathomimetics, MAOIs, neuromuscular blockers, bupropion.

## Contraindications
Porphyria, epilepsy or other convulsive disorders, perebral oedema, airways obstruction, cerebrovascular accident, severe hypertension & ischemic heart diseases.

## Side Effects
Dyspnoea, respiratory problems such as coughing bronchospasm. Other CNS side effects include convulsion, headache & dizziness. Cardiovascular side effects inlude Iteration of blood pressure & cardiac arrhythmia. Over dosage may cause nausea, vomiting & diarrhoea.

## Pregnancy & Lactation
There are no enough adequate and controlled studies in pregnant women and nursing mothers.

## Precautions & Warnings
It should not used in less severe degrees of hypertension or impaired cardiac reverse & hepatic dysfunction.

## Therapeutic Class
Respiratory stimulants: analeptics

## Storage Conditions
Store below 25°C in a dry place, away from light.
