## Indications
Napa Extend is indicated for the relief of mild to moderate pain including headache, migraine, toothache, backache, rheumatic and muscle pains, dysmenorrhoea, spondylitis and osteoarthritis, and for the reduction of fever. The extended-release formulation provides prolonged pain relief.

## Composition
Each extended-release tablet contains: Paracetamol BP 665 mg (immediate release 217.5 mg + extended release 447.5 mg). Excipients: Microcrystalline Cellulose, Hypromellose, Colloidal Anhydrous Silica, Magnesium Stearate.

## Pharmacology
Napa Extend is a modified-release formulation of paracetamol. The immediate-release portion provides rapid onset of action while the extended-release portion maintains plasma paracetamol concentrations over a longer period (up to 8 hours), allowing for twice or three-times daily dosing in chronic pain management. The analgesic and antipyretic mechanism is the same as conventional paracetamol through inhibition of prostaglandin synthesis at the CNS level.

## Dosage & Administration
Adults and children over 12 years: 2 tablets every 6-8 hours as required. Do not exceed 6 tablets (3990 mg) in 24 hours. Tablets should be swallowed whole and not chewed, crushed or split.

Not recommended for children under 12 years.

## Interaction
Same as conventional paracetamol. Enhanced anticoagulant effect with warfarin. Avoid concurrent alcohol use. Cholestyramine may reduce absorption.

## Contraindications
Contraindicated in patients with hypersensitivity to paracetamol, severe hepatic impairment, active peptic ulcer.

## Side Effects
Generally well tolerated. Rare: skin rash, urticaria, nausea. Serious hepatotoxicity with overdose.

## Pregnancy & Lactation
May be used during pregnancy and lactation at recommended doses. Consult a physician for prolonged use.

## Precautions & Warnings
Do not crush or chew the tablets as this destroys the extended-release mechanism. Monitor patients with hepatic impairment. Avoid in alcoholic patients. Do not use with other paracetamol-containing products.

## Overdose Effects
Similar to conventional paracetamol overdose. Liver damage may manifest 24-48 hours post-ingestion. Treat with N-acetylcysteine as antidote.

## Therapeutic Class
Non-opioid analgesic preparations

## Storage Conditions
Store below 30°C in a dry place, protected from moisture and light. Keep out of reach of children.
