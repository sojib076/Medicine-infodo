## Indications
Moxef is indicated for infections caused by susceptible aerobic and anaerobic Gram-positive and Gram-negative bacteria: Community-acquired pneumonia, Acute bacterial exacerbation of chronic bronchitis, Complicated and uncomplicated skin and soft tissue infections, Complicated intra-abdominal infections (in combination with metronidazole), Complicated urinary tract infections.

## Composition
Each film-coated tablet contains: Moxifloxacin Hydrochloride equivalent to Moxifloxacin 400 mg. Excipients: Microcrystalline Cellulose, Lactose Monohydrate, Croscarmellose Sodium, Magnesium Stearate, Hypromellose, Titanium Dioxide, Macrogol, Iron Oxide Red.

## Pharmacology
Moxifloxacin is a fourth-generation fluoroquinolone antibiotic. It acts by inhibiting bacterial DNA gyrase (topoisomerase II) and topoisomerase IV, enzymes essential for bacterial DNA replication, transcription, repair, and recombination. Moxifloxacin has a significantly broader spectrum than earlier fluoroquinolones, with enhanced activity against Gram-positive organisms (including MRSA with some activity), atypical pathogens, and anaerobes. It achieves very high tissue penetration (lung tissue concentrations 5-10 times higher than plasma). Bioavailability is approximately 91% and it has a half-life of 12 hours.

## Dosage & Administration
Adults: 400 mg once daily.
- Community-acquired pneumonia: 5-10 days.
- Acute bacterial exacerbation of COPD: 5 days.
- Uncomplicated skin infections: 7 days.
- Complicated skin and soft tissue infections: 7-21 days.
- Complicated intra-abdominal infections: 5-14 days.

Can be taken with or without food. Administer oral moxifloxacin at least 6 hours before or 2 hours after antacids containing magnesium or aluminium, sucralfate, or multivitamins containing iron or zinc.

## Interaction
QT prolongation with antiarrhythmics (amiodarone, sotalol), antipsychotics, TCAs, and other QT-prolonging drugs. NSAIDs may increase CNS stimulant effects. Antacids and iron/zinc preparations reduce absorption.

## Contraindications
Hypersensitivity to moxifloxacin or other fluoroquinolones. Pre-existing QT prolongation. Electrolyte disturbances (uncorrected hypokalaemia, hypomagnesaemia). Concomitant use with QT-prolonging drugs. Not recommended in children and adolescents, pregnancy, or breastfeeding due to cartilage and tendon effects.

## Side Effects
Common: nausea, diarrhoea, dizziness, headache. Uncommon: QT prolongation, vomiting, abdominal pain, elevated liver enzymes. Rare: hepatitis, seizures, psychosis, tendon rupture, peripheral neuropathy, photosensitivity.

## Pregnancy & Lactation
Contraindicated in pregnancy and lactation due to potential effects on fetal/neonatal cartilage and joints (fluoroquinolone class effect).

## Precautions & Warnings
ECG monitoring recommended in patients with risk factors for QT prolongation. Discontinue at first sign of tendinopathy. Avoid sun exposure (photosensitivity). Use with extreme caution in patients with seizure disorders or CNS pathology. Not for use in children.

## Overdose Effects
Limited data. Prolonged QT interval may occur. Supportive treatment and ECG monitoring recommended.

## Therapeutic Class
Fluoroquinolone antibacterial

## Storage Conditions
Store below 25°C, protected from light and moisture. Keep out of reach of children. Do not use after expiry date.
