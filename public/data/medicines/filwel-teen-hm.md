## Composition
Each film coated tablet contains: Vitamin A 2500 IU, Vitamin C 120 mg, Vitamin D 400 IU, Vitamin E 30 IU, Vitamin K 25 mcg, Thiamine 3.75 mg, Riboflavin 4.25 mg, Niacin 30 mg, Vitamin B6 5 mg, Folic Acid 400 mcg, Vitamin B12 15 mcg, Biotin 300 mcg, Pantothenic Acid 10 mg, Calcium 200 mg, Iron 9 mg, Magnesium 100 mg, Zinc 15 mg, Selenium 20 mcg, Copper 2 mg, Manganese 2 mg and Chromium 120 mcg.

## Dosage & Administration
For teen boys, one tablet daily with food.

## Interaction
No such drug interactions have been reported.

## Contraindications
Contraindicated in patients with a known hypersensitivity to any of the ingredients.

## Side Effects
Constipation, diarrhea or upset stomach may occur. These effects are usually temporary and may disappear as your body adjust to this medication. If any of these effects persist or worsen, contact with your doctor. Iron may cause your stools to turn black, an effect that is not harmful. 

Allergic sensitization has been reported following oral administration of Folic Acid.

## Precautions & Warnings
Folic Acid alone is improper therapy in the treatment of pernicious anaemia and other megaloblastic anaemia where Vitamin B12 is deficient.

## Therapeutic Class
Multi-vitamin & Multi-mineral combined preparations

## Storage Conditions
Keep this out of reach of children. Store in a cool (below 25° C) and dry place protected from light and moisture.
