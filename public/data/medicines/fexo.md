## Indications
Fexo is indicated for the relief of symptoms associated with: Seasonal allergic rhinitis (hay fever) – sneezing, runny nose, itchy and watery eyes, itching of the nose, throat or roof of mouth. Chronic idiopathic urticaria – pruritus and reduction of the number and size of urticaria weals.

## Composition
Each film-coated tablet contains: Fexofenadine Hydrochloride BP 120 mg. Excipients: Microcrystalline Cellulose, Croscarmellose Sodium, Povidone, Magnesium Stearate, Hypromellose, Titanium Dioxide, Colloidal Anhydrous Silica, Macrogol.

## Pharmacology
Fexofenadine is a selective peripheral H1-receptor antagonist (antihistamine). It competes with histamine for binding at peripheral H1-receptors on effector cells. Unlike older antihistamines, fexofenadine does not cross the blood-brain barrier significantly and is therefore non-sedating. It inhibits the release of inflammatory mediators from mast cells in vitro. Fexofenadine is rapidly absorbed following oral administration with a Tmax of 1-3 hours. It is not metabolised to any significant extent and is primarily eliminated via faeces.

## Dosage & Administration
Adults and adolescents over 12 years:
- Seasonal allergic rhinitis: 120 mg once daily.
- Chronic idiopathic urticaria: 120 mg once daily.

Take with water. Not to be taken with fruit juice (grapefruit, orange, or apple juice) as absorption may be reduced. Take at least 15 minutes before antacid preparations.

## Interaction
Erythromycin and ketoconazole increase plasma levels of fexofenadine (clinical significance unknown). Antacids containing aluminium and magnesium hydroxide decrease fexofenadine levels; separate dosing by at least 2 hours. Fruit juices (grapefruit, orange, apple) reduce bioavailability by up to 36%.

## Contraindications
Hypersensitivity to fexofenadine or any excipients.

## Side Effects
Common: headache, drowsiness, nausea, dizziness. Uncommon: fatigue, insomnia, nervousness, sleep disturbance, nightmares. Rare: tachycardia, palpitations, hypersensitivity reactions including anaphylaxis, angioedema, chest tightness, dyspnoea.

## Pregnancy & Lactation
Use during pregnancy only if clearly necessary. No adequate data in pregnant women. Excreted in breast milk in animal studies; breastfeeding not recommended during treatment.

## Precautions & Warnings
Use with caution in patients with renal impairment (reduce dose to 60 mg once daily). Avoid alcohol. Although non-sedating, individual responses vary; assess ability to drive before undertaking complex tasks.

## Overdose Effects
Dizziness, drowsiness, and dry mouth have been reported with doses up to 800 mg. Treatment is symptomatic and supportive.

## Therapeutic Class
Non-sedating antihistamine

## Storage Conditions
Store below 30°C in a dry place away from light. Keep out of reach of children.
