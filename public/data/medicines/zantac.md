## Indications
Zantac is indicated for: Duodenal ulcer (treatment and prevention of relapse), Gastric ulcer, Post-operative ulcer, Reflux oesophagitis, Zollinger-Ellison syndrome, Chronic episodic dyspepsia characterised by pain in the epigastrium or heartburn, Prophylaxis of haemorrhage from stress ulceration in seriously ill patients.

Note: Ranitidine-containing medicines were withdrawn in many countries due to NDMA contamination concerns. Availability in Bangladesh may vary; consult a pharmacist or physician for alternatives.

## Composition
Each tablet contains: Ranitidine Hydrochloride BP equivalent to Ranitidine 150 mg. Excipients: Microcrystalline Cellulose, Sodium Starch Glycolate, Magnesium Stearate, Hypromellose, Titanium Dioxide.

## Pharmacology
Ranitidine is a competitive and reversible antagonist at histamine H2-receptors on gastric parietal cells. By blocking H2 receptors, it reduces both basal and stimulated secretion of gastric acid and pepsin. A single 150 mg dose inhibits meal-stimulated acid secretion by 50% for 6-8 hours. Ranitidine is rapidly absorbed after oral administration (peak levels at 2-3 hours), with a bioavailability of approximately 50% due to first-pass metabolism. It is primarily excreted in urine.

## Dosage & Administration
Duodenal and gastric ulcer: 150 mg twice daily or 300 mg at bedtime for 4-8 weeks.
Maintenance: 150 mg at bedtime.
Reflux oesophagitis: 150 mg twice daily for 8 weeks; up to 600 mg daily in severe cases.
Zollinger-Ellison syndrome: 150 mg three times daily, adjustable up to 6 g/day.

## Interaction
May reduce absorption of ketoconazole and itraconazole (pH-dependent). May increase plasma levels of some drugs metabolised by CYP2D6. Antacids may reduce ranitidine absorption.

## Contraindications
Hypersensitivity to ranitidine or any H2-receptor antagonist.

## Side Effects
Generally well tolerated. Common: headache, diarrhoea, constipation, nausea. Uncommon: dizziness, somnolence, bradycardia, transient elevated liver enzymes. Rare: hepatitis, thrombocytopenia, gynaecomastia, alopecia, confusion.

## Pregnancy & Lactation
Ranitidine crosses the placenta but no teratogenic effects have been established. It is excreted in breast milk; use with caution during breastfeeding.

## Precautions & Warnings
Symptomatic response does not preclude gastric malignancy; exclude gastric cancer before initiating treatment. Reduce dose in renal impairment. Use with caution in hepatic impairment.

## Overdose Effects
Overdose is generally well tolerated. Muscular tremors, somnolence, and rapid breathing have been reported. Treat symptomatically.

## Therapeutic Class
H2-receptor antagonist

## Storage Conditions
Store below 25°C in a dry place, away from light. Keep out of reach of children.
