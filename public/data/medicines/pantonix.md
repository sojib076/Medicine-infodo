## Indications
Pantonix is indicated for: Gastro-oesophageal reflux disease (GORD) including erosive oesophagitis, Duodenal and gastric ulcers, Zollinger-Ellison syndrome and other hypersecretory conditions, Eradication of Helicobacter pylori in combination with antibiotics, Prevention of NSAID-induced gastroduodenal lesions in at-risk patients.

## Composition
Each enteric-coated tablet contains: Pantoprazole Sodium Sesquihydrate BP equivalent to Pantoprazole 40 mg. Excipients: Mannitol, Sodium Carbonate Anhydrous, Microcrystalline Cellulose, Povidone, Methacrylic Acid Copolymer, Hypromellose, Titanium Dioxide, Iron Oxide Yellow.

## Pharmacology
Pantoprazole is a proton pump inhibitor (PPI) that irreversibly inhibits the H+/K+-ATPase enzyme system in gastric parietal cells. Unlike omeprazole, pantoprazole has a higher pKa (3.9 vs 4.0) which leads to greater selectivity for the proton pump and fewer drug interactions. It is activated only in the highly acidic environment of the parietal cell secretory canaliculi. Pantoprazole is absorbed rapidly and reaches peak plasma levels within 2-3 hours. It is extensively metabolised by CYP2C19 with a half-life of 1 hour but the pharmacological effect persists for 24 hours.

## Dosage & Administration
GORD: 40 mg once daily for 4-8 weeks.
Duodenal ulcer: 40 mg once daily for 4 weeks.
Gastric ulcer: 40 mg once daily for 8 weeks.
Zollinger-Ellison syndrome: 80-160 mg/day in divided doses.
H. pylori eradication: 40 mg twice daily for 7-14 days with antibiotics.

Best taken 30-60 minutes before the first meal of the day. Swallow whole; do not crush or chew.

## Interaction
May reduce absorption of drugs whose bioavailability is pH-dependent (ketoconazole, itraconazole, erlotinib, iron). Less interaction with CYP2C19 substrates compared to omeprazole. May increase methotrexate levels. No significant interaction with anticoagulants.

## Contraindications
Hypersensitivity to pantoprazole, substituted benzimidazoles, or any excipients. Concomitant use with HIV protease inhibitors.

## Side Effects
Common: diarrhoea, nausea, abdominal pain, headache, flatulence. Uncommon: insomnia, dizziness. Rare: hypomagnesaemia, bone fractures with long-term use, C. difficile-associated diarrhoea.

## Pregnancy & Lactation
Use during pregnancy only if clearly needed as animal studies showed embryotoxicity at high doses. Small amounts excreted in breast milk; avoid during lactation unless essential.

## Precautions & Warnings
Exclude malignancy before treating gastric ulcer symptoms. Long-term use may reduce magnesium levels and increase fracture risk. CYP2C19 poor metabolisers may have higher plasma levels. Do not prescribe for trivial symptoms; use lowest effective dose for shortest duration.

## Overdose Effects
No specific information on overdosage. Doses up to 240 mg IV have been administered without adverse effects. Treatment is symptomatic and supportive.

## Therapeutic Class
Proton pump inhibitor

## Storage Conditions
Store below 25°C, protected from light and moisture. Keep in the original container. Keep out of reach of children.
