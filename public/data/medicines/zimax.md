## Indications
Zimax is indicated for mild to moderate infections caused by susceptible organisms: Community-acquired pneumonia (atypical pathogens), Acute bacterial exacerbation of chronic bronchitis, Acute bacterial sinusitis, Pharyngitis/tonsillitis (as an alternative when first-line therapy is not appropriate), Uncomplicated skin and soft tissue infections, Uncomplicated urethritis and cervicitis due to Chlamydia trachomatis.

## Composition
Each tablet contains: Azithromycin Dihydrate BP equivalent to Azithromycin 250 mg. Excipients: Microcrystalline Cellulose, Sodium Starch Glycolate, Magnesium Stearate, Hydroxypropylmethylcellulose, Titanium Dioxide, Macrogol.

## Pharmacology
Azithromycin, as in the 500 mg formulation, acts by binding to the 50S ribosomal subunit and inhibiting RNA-dependent protein synthesis. The 250 mg dose achieves similar tissue concentrations to 500 mg due to the prolonged tissue accumulation of azithromycin (tissue half-life 68-76 hours). This allows a 5-day treatment course with equivalent efficacy to longer courses of other antibiotics.

## Dosage & Administration
Adults and children over 45 kg:
- Standard 5-day course: 500 mg (2 x 250 mg) on Day 1, then 250 mg once daily on Days 2-5.
- Community-acquired pneumonia: 500 mg on Day 1, then 250 mg once daily for 4 days.
- Chlamydial infection: 1 g (4 x 250 mg) as a single dose.

Take 1 hour before or 2 hours after meals.

## Interaction
See Azimax 500 mg. Antacids reduce peak plasma levels. May prolong QT interval. Warfarin effect may be enhanced.

## Contraindications
Hypersensitivity to azithromycin or other macrolide antibiotics. History of cholestatic jaundice/hepatic dysfunction with prior azithromycin use.

## Side Effects
Common: nausea, abdominal discomfort, diarrhoea, loose stools, vomiting. Uncommon: headache, dizziness, somnolence, taste disturbance. Rare: hepatotoxicity, QT prolongation, pseudomembranous colitis.

## Pregnancy & Lactation
Use only if clearly necessary. Not recommended during breastfeeding without medical advice.

## Precautions & Warnings
Use with caution in hepatic impairment, QT-prolonging conditions. Antibiotic resistance may develop with inappropriate use.

## Overdose Effects
Nausea, vomiting, diarrhoea reported. Reversible hearing loss has been associated with high doses. Treatment is symptomatic.

## Therapeutic Class
Macrolide antibiotics

## Storage Conditions
Store below 30°C in a dry place, protected from light. Keep out of reach of children.
