## Indications
Seclo is indicated for: Duodenal ulcer, Gastric ulcer, Gastro-oesophageal reflux disease (GORD), Erosive or ulcerative oesophagitis, Zollinger-Ellison syndrome, Helicobacter pylori eradication (in combination with antibiotics), NSAID-associated gastric and duodenal ulcers and their prevention.

## Composition
Each capsule contains: Omeprazole BP 20 mg as enteric-coated pellets. Excipients: Sugar spheres, Hypromellose, Dibutyl Sebacate, Methacrylic Acid Copolymer, Triethyl Citrate, Talc.

## Pharmacology
Omeprazole is a proton pump inhibitor (PPI). It acts by specifically inhibiting the H+/K+-ATPase enzyme system (proton pump) at the secretory surface of the gastric parietal cell, thereby blocking the final step in acid secretion. It is a prodrug activated by acid in the secretory canaliculi of the parietal cell. A single daily dose of 20 mg produces over 90% inhibition of 24-hour acid secretion. Omeprazole is completely absorbed from the small intestine. It is extensively metabolised in the liver by CYP2C19 and CYP3A4, with an elimination half-life of 0.5-1 hour.

## Dosage & Administration
Duodenal ulcer: 20 mg once daily for 4 weeks; most patients heal within 4 weeks.
Gastric ulcer and oesophagitis: 20 mg once daily for 4-8 weeks.
GORD maintenance: 10-20 mg once daily.
H. pylori eradication: 20 mg twice daily for 7 days combined with appropriate antibiotics.
Zollinger-Ellison syndrome: Starting dose 60 mg once daily, adjusted as needed.

Best taken 30 minutes before meals.

## Interaction
Omeprazole may reduce absorption of drugs dependent on gastric pH (ketoconazole, itraconazole, iron salts, ampicillin). May increase plasma levels of diazepam, phenytoin, and warfarin. Clopidogrel antiplatelet effect may be reduced.

## Contraindications
Hypersensitivity to omeprazole, substituted benzimidazoles, or excipients. Concomitant use with nelfinavir.

## Side Effects
Common: headache, diarrhoea, nausea, vomiting, flatulence, abdominal pain, constipation. Uncommon: dry mouth, peripheral oedema. Rare: hypomagnesaemia with prolonged use, C. difficile colitis. Very rare: agranulocytosis, hepatitis.

## Pregnancy & Lactation
Omeprazole should be used in pregnancy only if the potential benefit outweighs the risk. It is excreted in breast milk; not recommended during lactation unless essential.

## Precautions & Warnings
Long-term use (over 1 year) may increase risk of hip, wrist, and spine fractures. Monitor magnesium levels in patients on prolonged therapy. Rule out malignancy before treating gastric ulcers. May mask symptoms of gastric cancer.

## Overdose Effects
Single doses of up to 160 mg have been ingested without significant adverse effects. No specific antidote. Treatment is symptomatic.

## Therapeutic Class
Proton pump inhibitor

## Storage Conditions
Store below 25°C in a dry place away from light. Protect from moisture. Keep out of reach of children.
