## Indications
Amoxil is indicated for the treatment of infections caused by susceptible strains of bacteria: Upper respiratory tract infections (otitis media, sinusitis, pharyngitis, tonsillitis), Lower respiratory tract infections (community-acquired pneumonia, acute exacerbation of chronic bronchitis), Urinary tract infections (uncomplicated cystitis), Skin and soft tissue infections, Dental infections, H. pylori eradication (triple therapy), Prophylaxis of bacterial endocarditis.

## Composition
Each capsule contains: Amoxicillin Trihydrate BP equivalent to Amoxicillin 250 mg. Excipients: Magnesium Stearate, Colloidal Silicon Dioxide.

## Pharmacology
Amoxicillin is a broad-spectrum aminopenicillin antibiotic. It is bactericidal and acts by inhibiting bacterial cell wall synthesis by binding to penicillin-binding proteins (PBPs). It is susceptible to beta-lactamase producing organisms. Amoxicillin is well absorbed (75-90% bioavailability) from the GIT and is not significantly affected by food. It is widely distributed in body tissues and fluids. Amoxicillin is partly metabolised (by hydrolysis of the beta-lactam ring) and excreted mainly unchanged in urine.

## Dosage & Administration
Adults and children over 40 kg:
- Mild to moderate infections: 250 mg every 8 hours or 500 mg every 12 hours.
- Severe infections: 500 mg every 8 hours.
- H. pylori eradication: 1 g twice daily for 7 days with PPI and clarithromycin.

Children under 40 kg: 25-45 mg/kg/day in divided doses.

## Interaction
Concurrent use with probenecid increases serum amoxicillin levels. Methotrexate toxicity may be increased. Oral anticoagulants effect may be enhanced. Tetracyclines and chloramphenicol may interfere with bactericidal activity.

## Contraindications
Hypersensitivity to amoxicillin, any penicillin, or excipients. History of immediate hypersensitivity to beta-lactam antibiotics.

## Side Effects
Common: diarrhoea, nausea, skin rash. Uncommon: vomiting, headache, urticaria. Rare: anaphylaxis, pseudomembranous colitis, Stevens-Johnson syndrome, hepatitis. Maculopapular rash common in patients with viral infections (especially EBV).

## Pregnancy & Lactation
Amoxicillin is considered safe during pregnancy. It is excreted in small amounts in breast milk; use with caution and monitor the infant for possible sensitisation.

## Precautions & Warnings
Perform culture and sensitivity testing before initiating therapy. Prolonged use may lead to superinfection. Cross-sensitivity with cephalosporins (approximately 1-10%). Test for penicillin hypersensitivity in patients with history of allergy.

## Overdose Effects
Nausea, vomiting, diarrhoea, and disturbances of electrolyte balance may occur. In renal impairment, crystalluria and nephropathy may occur. Haemodialysis removes amoxicillin.

## Therapeutic Class
Aminopenicillin antibiotic

## Storage Conditions
Store below 25°C in a dry place away from moisture. Keep out of reach of children.
