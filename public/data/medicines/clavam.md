## Indications
Clavam is indicated for infections caused by beta-lactamase-producing strains that are resistant to amoxicillin alone: Lower respiratory tract infections (community-acquired pneumonia, acute exacerbation of chronic bronchitis), Urinary tract infections (complicated), Skin and soft tissue infections, Otitis media and sinusitis, Dental infections, Bone and joint infections, Intra-abdominal infections.

## Composition
Each film-coated tablet contains: Amoxicillin Trihydrate BP equivalent to Amoxicillin 500 mg and Clavulanate Potassium BP equivalent to Clavulanic Acid 125 mg (625 mg total). Excipients: Microcrystalline Cellulose, Crospovidone, Magnesium Stearate, Hypromellose, Titanium Dioxide, Colloidal Silicon Dioxide.

## Pharmacology
Clavam (Co-amoxiclav) combines amoxicillin (bactericidal aminopenicillin) with clavulanic acid (beta-lactamase inhibitor). Clavulanic acid is a suicide inhibitor that binds irreversibly to bacterial beta-lactamases, protecting amoxicillin from enzymatic degradation. This combination extends the spectrum of amoxicillin to include beta-lactamase-producing strains of H. influenzae, M. catarrhalis, S. aureus, E. coli, Klebsiella, and Bacteroides fragilis. Both components are well absorbed orally with similar pharmacokinetics.

## Dosage & Administration
Adults and children over 40 kg:
- Standard dose: One 625 mg tablet every 8 hours (for 5-14 days depending on infection).
- Dental infections: 625 mg every 8 hours for 5 days.
- Severe infections or respiratory: 625 mg every 8 hours or 1 g (875 mg/125 mg) every 12 hours.

Take at the start of a meal to reduce GI effects.

## Interaction
Probenecid increases amoxicillin levels. Anticoagulants effect enhanced. Allopurinol increases risk of skin rash. Mycophenolate mofetil levels may be reduced.

## Contraindications
Hypersensitivity to amoxicillin, clavulanate, any penicillin, or excipients. History of co-amoxiclav-associated cholestatic jaundice or hepatic dysfunction. Mononucleosis.

## Side Effects
Common: diarrhoea, nausea, candidiasis. Uncommon: vomiting, indigestion, skin rash, urticaria, cholestatic jaundice. Rare: hepatitis, erythema multiforme, pseudomembranous colitis, anaphylaxis.

## Pregnancy & Lactation
Use only if clearly indicated. There is a small risk of neonatal necrotising enterocolitis if used in preterm rupture of membranes. Excreted in breast milk; monitor infant.

## Precautions & Warnings
Take with food to reduce GI side effects. Monitor liver function in patients with hepatic disease. Risk of antibiotic-associated colitis. Assess penicillin hypersensitivity before prescribing.

## Overdose Effects
Nausea, vomiting, diarrhoea, disturbance of fluid-electrolyte balance. Haemodialysis removes co-amoxiclav from circulation.

## Therapeutic Class
Aminopenicillin + beta-lactamase inhibitor antibiotic

## Storage Conditions
Store below 25°C in a dry place, away from moisture. Protect from light. Keep out of reach of children.
