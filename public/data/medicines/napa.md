## Indications
Napa is indicated for the relief of mild to moderate pain including headache, migraine, toothache, backache, rheumatic and muscle pains, dysmenorrhoea, and for the reduction of fever including post-immunisation fever.

## Composition
Each tablet contains: Paracetamol BP 500 mg. Excipients: Starch, Microcrystalline Cellulose, Povidone, Sodium Starch Glycolate, Magnesium Stearate.

## Pharmacology
Paracetamol has analgesic and antipyretic properties with weak anti-inflammatory activity. The analgesic mechanism of paracetamol is not fully elucidated. Paracetamol is thought to act via inhibition of prostaglandin synthesis, particularly in the CNS, thus raising the pain threshold. The antipyretic effect is produced by acting on the hypothalamic heat-regulating centre to produce vasodilation and increased peripheral blood flow, thereby increasing the dissipation of excess heat.

Paracetamol is rapidly and completely absorbed from the GIT. Peak plasma concentrations occur about 30 to 60 minutes after oral dosing. Paracetamol is distributed throughout most body fluids and is metabolised mainly by the liver. It is excreted in the urine mainly as glucuronide and sulphate conjugates.

## Dosage & Administration
Adults and children over 12 years: 1-2 tablets every 4-6 hours as required. Maximum 8 tablets in 24 hours.

Children 6-12 years: Half to one tablet every 4-6 hours as required. Maximum 4 tablets in 24 hours.

Children under 6 years: Not recommended in tablet form; liquid preparations are preferable.

Do not take more than the recommended dose. If symptoms persist consult a physician.

## Interaction
Paracetamol may enhance the anticoagulant effect of warfarin and other coumarins. Regular use of large doses of paracetamol may increase the risk of bleeding. Concurrent use with other hepatotoxic drugs may increase the risk of liver damage.

## Contraindications
Napa is contraindicated in patients with known hypersensitivity to paracetamol or any of the excipients.

## Side Effects
Paracetamol is well tolerated at therapeutic doses. Adverse effects are rare but may include: skin rash, blood disorders including thrombocytopenia, leukopenia and agranulocytosis. Liver damage and less frequently renal damage may occur following overdose.

## Pregnancy & Lactation
Paracetamol has been widely used during all stages of pregnancy. Available data do not indicate any adverse effects on the mother or fetus. Paracetamol is excreted in breast milk but in amounts too small to be harmful to the nursing infant.

## Precautions & Warnings
Care should be taken when administering paracetamol to patients with hepatic or renal impairment. Alcohol use increases the risk of hepatotoxicity. Do not exceed the recommended dosage. Avoid concurrent use with other paracetamol-containing products.

## Overdose Effects
Symptoms of overdose include nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and general malaise. Liver damage may occur 24-48 hours after ingestion. Treatment: Gastric lavage should be performed if indicated. N-acetylcysteine (IV or oral) is the specific antidote.

## Therapeutic Class
Non-opioid analgesic preparations

## Storage Conditions
Store below 30°C in a dry place away from light. Keep out of reach of children. Do not use after the expiry date.
