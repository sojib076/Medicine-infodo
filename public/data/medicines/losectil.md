## Indications
Losectil is indicated for: Duodenal ulcer, Benign gastric ulcer, Reflux oesophagitis, Zollinger-Ellison syndrome, Eradication of Helicobacter pylori (in combination with antibiotics), Long-term management of patients who have responded to treatment of reflux oesophagitis.

## Composition
Each capsule contains: Omeprazole BP 40 mg as enteric-coated pellets. Excipients: Sugar spheres, Hypromellose, Methacrylic Acid-Ethyl Acrylate Copolymer (1:1), Dibutyl Phthalate, Talc.

## Pharmacology
Omeprazole 40 mg provides maximum acid suppression for patients with severe oesophagitis or Zollinger-Ellison syndrome. It is a substituted benzimidazole proton pump inhibitor that specifically inhibits the final step in gastric acid secretion at the H+/K+-ATPase enzyme. The antisecretory effect lasts more than 24 hours after a single dose. The drug accumulates in parietal cell canaliculi and is activated by the acidic pH to a sulphenamide form that covalently binds to cysteine residues of the proton pump.

## Dosage & Administration
Severe oesophagitis or GORD: 40 mg once daily for 4-8 weeks; maintenance 20 mg once daily.
Duodenal ulcer: 20 mg once daily for 4 weeks (40 mg for resistant cases).
Zollinger-Ellison syndrome: 60 mg once daily initially; up to 120 mg in divided doses.
H. pylori eradication: 40 mg twice daily for 7 days with clarithromycin 500 mg + amoxicillin 1 g twice daily.

Best taken 30 minutes before the first meal of the day.

## Interaction
Reduces absorption of pH-dependent drugs. Increases plasma levels of diazepam, phenytoin, and R-warfarin via CYP2C19 inhibition. Reduces clopidogrel effectiveness. May increase levels of methotrexate.

## Contraindications
Hypersensitivity to omeprazole, substituted benzimidazoles, or any component of the formulation.

## Side Effects
Common: headache, diarrhoea, nausea, abdominal pain, flatulence, constipation. Uncommon: taste alteration, peripheral oedema, increased liver enzymes. Rare: Stevens-Johnson syndrome, hypomagnesaemia, subacute cutaneous lupus erythematosus.

## Pregnancy & Lactation
Reproductive toxicity studies in animals show no harm at therapeutic doses. However, use during pregnancy only when clearly necessary. Passes into breast milk; avoid during breastfeeding.

## Precautions & Warnings
Rule out gastric malignancy before initiating therapy for gastric ulcer. Prolonged therapy may lead to Clostridium difficile infection. Monitor for hypomagnesaemia and bone density in long-term users.

## Overdose Effects
Overdosage experience is limited. Reported symptoms: confusion, blurred vision, tachycardia, nausea, diaphoresis. Treatment is symptomatic.

## Therapeutic Class
Proton pump inhibitor

## Storage Conditions
Store below 30°C, protected from light and moisture. Keep in tightly closed container. Keep out of reach of children.
