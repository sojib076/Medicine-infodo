## Indications
Cetrizin is indicated for the symptomatic relief of: Seasonal and perennial allergic rhinitis (sneezing, rhinorrhoea, nasal pruritus, nasal congestion, watery eyes), Allergic conjunctivitis, Chronic idiopathic urticaria, Pruritus associated with atopic dermatitis.

## Composition
Each film-coated tablet contains: Cetirizine Hydrochloride BP 10 mg. Excipients: Lactose Monohydrate, Microcrystalline Cellulose, Starch, Povidone, Magnesium Stearate, Hypromellose, Titanium Dioxide, Macrogol.

## Pharmacology
Cetirizine is a second-generation, non-sedating antihistamine. It is a selective antagonist at peripheral H1-receptor sites and an inhibitor of the eosinophil chemotaxis. It has minimal CNS penetration due to its zwitterionic nature and low lipophilicity. Cetirizine also inhibits the late-phase allergic response by inhibiting eosinophil migration and reducing the expression of adhesion molecules. It is rapidly absorbed with peak plasma levels at 1 hour, and is primarily excreted unchanged in urine.

## Dosage & Administration
Adults and children over 12 years: 10 mg once daily in the evening.

Children 6-12 years: 5 mg twice daily or 10 mg once daily.

Children 2-6 years: 2.5 mg twice daily (oral solution preferred).

Patients with renal impairment: Dose reduction required based on creatinine clearance.

## Interaction
CNS depressants and alcohol: increased sedation. Theophylline (1 g/day): slight decrease in cetirizine clearance. No significant CYP interactions.

## Contraindications
Hypersensitivity to cetirizine, hydroxyzine, or any excipients. Severe renal impairment (CrCl < 10 mL/min). Not recommended in children under 2 years for the tablet form.

## Side Effects
Common: somnolence (more common at higher doses), headache, dry mouth, fatigue. Uncommon: dizziness, abdominal pain, nausea. Rare: aggression, convulsions, tachycardia, hypersensitivity reactions.

## Pregnancy & Lactation
Use during pregnancy only if clearly necessary. Cetirizine is excreted in breast milk; breastfeeding should be avoided during treatment.

## Precautions & Warnings
Although less sedating than first-generation antihistamines, some patients may experience drowsiness particularly at higher doses. Caution when driving or operating machinery. Reduce dose in renal or hepatic impairment. Avoid alcohol.

## Overdose Effects
Somnolence and urinary retention have been reported in overdose. Treatment is symptomatic. Haemodialysis is not effective in removing cetirizine.

## Therapeutic Class
Non-sedating antihistamine

## Storage Conditions
Store below 30°C in a dry place, away from light. Keep out of reach of children.
