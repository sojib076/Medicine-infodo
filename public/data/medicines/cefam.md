## Indications
Cefam is indicated for infections caused by susceptible organisms: Upper and lower respiratory tract infections, Otitis media, Skin and soft tissue infections, Urinary tract infections, Bone and joint infections, Prophylaxis before surgical procedures.

## Composition
Each capsule contains: Cephalexin Monohydrate USP equivalent to Cephalexin 500 mg. Excipients: Magnesium Stearate, Talc, Gelatin (capsule shell), Titanium Dioxide.

## Pharmacology
Cephalexin is a first-generation oral cephalosporin. It acts by inhibiting bacterial cell wall synthesis through binding to penicillin-binding proteins (PBPs), particularly PBP-1. It has good activity against most Gram-positive organisms (Staphylococcus aureus, Streptococcus) and limited Gram-negative coverage (E. coli, Klebsiella, Proteus mirabilis). Cephalexin is well absorbed orally with >90% bioavailability, not affected by food. It is not metabolised and is excreted unchanged in urine, making it effective for urinary tract infections.

## Dosage & Administration
Adults:
- Mild infections: 250 mg every 6 hours.
- Moderate infections: 500 mg every 8-12 hours.
- Severe infections: 500 mg every 6 hours.
- UTI: 500 mg twice daily for 7-14 days.
- Surgical prophylaxis: 1-2 g 30-60 minutes before procedure.

Children: 25-100 mg/kg/day in 4 divided doses.

## Interaction
Probenecid reduces renal tubular secretion of cephalexin, increasing plasma levels. Metformin: cephalexin may increase metformin levels by reducing renal clearance. Anticoagulant effect of warfarin may be enhanced.

## Contraindications
Known hypersensitivity to cephalexin or cephalosporins. Caution in penicillin-allergic patients (cross-hypersensitivity 1-10%).

## Side Effects
Common: diarrhoea, nausea, abdominal pain, dyspepsia. Uncommon: skin rash, urticaria, headache, agitation, confusion. Rare: pseudomembranous colitis, anaphylaxis, hepatotoxicity, haemolytic anaemia.

## Pregnancy & Lactation
Considered safe during pregnancy. Excreted in small amounts in breast milk; use with caution and monitor infant.

## Precautions & Warnings
Use with caution in patients with penicillin allergy. Reduce dose in renal impairment. Prolonged use may lead to superinfection with resistant organisms. Positive Coombs test may occur.

## Overdose Effects
Nausea, vomiting, epigastric distress, diarrhoea, haematuria possible in overdose. Treat symptomatically. Haemodialysis can reduce serum levels.

## Therapeutic Class
First generation cephalosporin antibiotic

## Storage Conditions
Store below 25°C in a dry place, away from moisture and heat. Keep out of reach of children.
