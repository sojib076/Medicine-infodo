## Indications
Azimax is indicated for the treatment of mild to moderately severe infections caused by susceptible organisms: Community-acquired pneumonia, Acute bacterial exacerbation of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), Acute bacterial sinusitis, Pharyngitis/tonsillitis caused by Streptococcus pyogenes, Uncomplicated skin and skin structure infections, Urethritis and cervicitis caused by Chlamydia trachomatis or Neisseria gonorrhoeae, Genital ulcer disease in men (due to Haemophilus ducreyi).

## Composition
Each film-coated tablet contains: Azithromycin Dihydrate BP equivalent to Azithromycin 500 mg. Excipients: Microcrystalline Cellulose, Dibasic Calcium Phosphate, Sodium Croscarmellose, Magnesium Stearate, Hydroxypropyl Methylcellulose, Titanium Dioxide.

## Pharmacology
Azithromycin is a macrolide antibiotic. It acts by binding to the 50S ribosomal subunit of susceptible bacteria and inhibiting RNA-dependent protein synthesis. It achieves high tissue concentrations and has a prolonged half-life (68 hours), allowing once-daily dosing with a short course of treatment. Azithromycin is rapidly absorbed from the GIT and widely distributed throughout the body. Its bioavailability is approximately 37%. It is metabolised in the liver and excreted primarily in bile.

## Dosage & Administration
Adults:
- Community-acquired pneumonia: 500 mg once daily for 3 days, or 500 mg on Day 1 followed by 250 mg once daily on Days 2-5.
- Pharyngitis/tonsillitis: 500 mg once daily for 3 days.
- Skin and soft tissue infections: 500 mg once daily for 3 days.
- Chlamydial urethritis/cervicitis: 1 g single dose.
- Gonorrhoea: 2 g single dose (in combination therapy).

Take at least 1 hour before or 2 hours after meals.

## Interaction
Antacids containing aluminium or magnesium reduce azithromycin peak plasma levels; take 1 hour before or 2 hours after antacids. May potentiate anticoagulant effect of warfarin. Concurrent use with ergot alkaloids not recommended. May prolong QT interval; caution with other QT-prolonging drugs.

## Contraindications
Hypersensitivity to azithromycin, erythromycin, or any macrolide antibiotic. History of cholestatic jaundice/hepatic dysfunction associated with prior azithromycin use.

## Side Effects
Common: diarrhoea, nausea, abdominal pain, vomiting. Uncommon: headache, dizziness, taste disturbance. Rare: hepatotoxicity, QT prolongation, Clostridium difficile-associated diarrhoea, hypersensitivity reactions including anaphylaxis.

## Pregnancy & Lactation
Should be used during pregnancy only if clearly needed. It is not known whether azithromycin passes into breast milk in sufficient amounts to affect the nursing infant; caution is advised.

## Precautions & Warnings
Use with caution in patients with hepatic impairment. Monitor cardiac patients as QT prolongation has been reported. Inform patients about symptoms of superinfection. Prolonged or repeated use may result in overgrowth of non-susceptible organisms.

## Overdose Effects
Nausea, vomiting, diarrhoea, and reversible hearing loss have been reported. Treatment is symptomatic and supportive. Gastric lavage may be considered.

## Therapeutic Class
Macrolide antibiotics

## Storage Conditions
Store below 30°C in a dry place, away from direct sunlight. Keep out of reach of children.
